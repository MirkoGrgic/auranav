Travel Insurance Explained

Understand what travel insurance actually covers, what it doesn't, and how to choose the right protection before your next adventure.

5 min read Worldwide Estimated Cost: $300

Why travel insurance matters

Many travelers see travel insurance as an unnecessary expense, but unexpected situations can quickly become expensive. Medical emergencies, canceled flights, lost luggage, and trip interruptions can cost far more than the insurance itself.

AuraNav tip: Travel insurance is often most valuable for medical emergencies abroad, not just flight cancellations.

Medical coverage

Medical protection is usually the most important part of any policy. Healthcare costs can be extremely expensive in some countries, especially if hospitalization or emergency transportation is required.

Trip cancellation protection

This coverage may reimburse prepaid travel expenses if you need to cancel your trip for a covered reason such as illness, injury, or certain emergencies.

Lost or delayed baggage

Many policies include compensation if luggage is delayed, damaged, or lost during transit.

Emergency assistance

Most insurers provide 24/7 emergency assistance services, helping travelers find hospitals, arrange transportation, or receive support during emergencies.

What insurance usually doesn't cover

Policies often exclude pre-existing medical conditions, extreme sports, risky activities, and cancellations that do not meet the policy's requirements.

Before buying a policy

  • Read coverage limits carefully.
  • Check medical coverage amounts.
  • Review exclusions and restrictions.
  • Compare multiple providers.
  • Keep digital copies of policy documents.

Final Thoughts

Travel insurance provides peace of mind and financial protection. While nobody plans for problems during a trip, having the right coverage can make a major difference when unexpected situations occur.
