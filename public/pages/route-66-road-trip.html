Road Trips

Exploring Route 66

Travel across America's most legendary highway and experience classic diners, desert landscapes, historic towns, neon signs, and the spirit of the open road.

12 min read USA Estimated Cost: $2,800

The Mother Road of America

Route 66 stretches from Chicago to Los Angeles and remains one of the world's most famous road trips. Although parts of the original route have been replaced by modern highways, much of its historic charm remains.

Along the journey you'll find vintage motels, roadside attractions, small-town America, and landscapes that helped define American road trip culture.

AuraNav tip: Don't rush. The magic of Route 66 comes from exploring small towns and unexpected roadside attractions.

Chicago: The Starting Point

Most travelers begin in Chicago, where Route 66 officially starts. Spend a day exploring the city before heading west toward the open road.

Missouri and Historic Americana

Small towns, vintage diners, and classic Route 66 museums provide a glimpse into America's golden age of road travel.

Oklahoma and Texas

Vast plains and iconic roadside stops dominate this section of the route. Expect giant signs, quirky attractions, and endless horizons.

New Mexico Landscapes

Route 66 passes through desert scenery, historic pueblos, and colorful towns such as Santa Fe and Albuquerque.

Arizona Highlights

Arizona contains some of the most famous Route 66 segments, including Seligman, Winslow, and easy access to the Grand Canyon.

Arriving in California

The final section crosses the Mojave Desert before ending near Santa Monica Pier on the Pacific Coast.

Road Trip Tips

  • Plan at least 10–14 days for the full route.
  • Visit roadside museums and diners.
  • Book accommodations in advance during summer.
  • Bring a camera for classic Americana moments.
  • Allow time for spontaneous detours.

Final Thoughts

Route 66 is more than a highway—it's a journey through American history, culture, and landscapes. Every stop tells a different story and makes the adventure unforgettable.
