Budget Tips

Cheap Eats in Rome

Enjoy authentic Italian food without tourist prices. Discover local pizza spots, family-run trattorias, and affordable Roman classics.

4 min read Rome Estimated Cost: $1,100

Eating well in Rome doesn't require a huge budget

Rome is famous for incredible food, but many visitors end up spending far more than necessary because they eat near major attractions. The truth is that some of the city's best meals are found away from tourist-heavy streets.

Traditional Roman dishes are often simple and affordable, making Rome one of the easiest European capitals for food lovers traveling on a budget.

AuraNav tip: Avoid restaurants with large tourist menus and aggressive street promoters. Walk two or three streets away from major landmarks for better prices and better food.

Grab pizza by the slice

Pizza al taglio is one of Rome's best-value meals. You pay by weight, allowing you to try several different flavors without spending much.

Look for lunch menus

Many local restaurants offer fixed-price lunch menus that include pasta, drinks, and sometimes dessert for significantly less than dinner prices.

Try Roman street food

Supplì, panini, fresh pastries, and local snacks are affordable, filling, and widely available throughout the city.

Explore neighborhood trattorias

Areas such as Trastevere and Testaccio offer authentic Roman dining experiences with more local customers and fewer tourist markups.

Must-try dishes

  • Cacio e Pepe
  • Carbonara
  • Amatriciana
  • Supplì
  • Roman-style pizza

Final thoughts

Rome proves that incredible food doesn't have to be expensive. With a little local knowledge, travelers can enjoy authentic Italian cuisine while staying comfortably within budget.
