Hiking the Inca Trail to Machu Picchu

Follow one of the world's most legendary trekking routes through mountain passes, cloud forests, ancient ruins, and breathtaking Andean landscapes before arriving at Machu Picchu.

14 min read Peru Estimated Cost: $2,800

Why the Inca Trail is unforgettable

Few hiking experiences compare to the Inca Trail. Instead of arriving at Machu Picchu by train, hikers gradually earn the view after several days of trekking through remote mountain scenery and ancient archaeological sites.

The trail combines physical challenge, natural beauty, and centuries of history into a single journey that remains on many travelers' bucket lists.

AuraNav tip: Book permits several months in advance. Inca Trail availability is limited and often sells out quickly.

Prepare for high altitude

One of the biggest challenges is altitude. Cusco sits over 3,300 meters above sea level, and parts of the trail climb even higher. Spending a few days acclimatizing before the trek can greatly improve your experience.

Dead Woman's Pass

The highest point of the classic route is Dead Woman's Pass, reaching approximately 4,215 meters. While demanding, the views and sense of achievement make it one of the trek's highlights.

Ancient Inca ruins along the route

Unlike many mountain hikes, the Inca Trail includes multiple archaeological sites scattered throughout the route. These ruins offer insight into the engineering and culture of the Inca civilization.

Sun Gate arrival

The final morning brings hikers to Inti Punku, also known as the Sun Gate. This is where many travelers get their first panoramic view of Machu Picchu.

What to pack

Weather conditions can change quickly in the Andes. Layered clothing, waterproof gear, hiking boots, sunscreen, and hydration systems are essential.

Trekking tips

  • Arrive in Cusco several days early.
  • Train beforehand with longer hikes.
  • Carry only essential gear.
  • Stay hydrated throughout the trek.
  • Choose a reputable trekking operator.

Final thoughts

Hiking the Inca Trail is much more than reaching Machu Picchu. The journey itself becomes the highlight, combining adventure, history, and some of South America's most spectacular scenery.
